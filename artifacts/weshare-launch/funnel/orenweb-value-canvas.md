## Part 1 — OrenWeb Value-Proposition Canvas

The one service with locked pricing: **$997 setup + $247/mo**, live in **five days or less**, sold mockup-first by Sales Partners. This canvas tests whether that offer actually maps to the buyer's life. It does — and here is the honest map, not the flattering one.

### Customer profile — local service business owner (trades, clinics, shops, solo operators)

| Jobs to be done | Pains (current reality) | Gains (what "good" looks like) |
|---|---|---|
| Get found when a local customer searches | Invisible on Google — the customer who searched found a competitor instead | Show up looking legitimate when it counts |
| Look like a real business before the first call | Last web guy took a deposit, went quiet, left a half-finished site | A finished site, live fast, that someone else keeps running |
| Turn searches into calls and booked jobs | Agency quotes: $3,000–$8,000, "8–12 weeks," a contract full of jargon | One fixed price they can say out loud without wincing |
| Never touch code, hosting, or "the backend" | Paying for a promise — money first, results maybe | See the thing before paying for the thing |
| Keep the monthly cost predictable | DIY builders half-finished at 11 p.m., abandoned | It just works, and stays working, without them |

**Current workaround** (the Osterwalder chain demands it): nothing, a dead Facebook page, or an abandoned DIY draft. The workaround is *doing without* — which is why the pain compounds monthly and the sale is winnable in one conversation.

### Value map — the actual offer, nothing more

| Products & services | Pain relievers | Gain creators |
|---|---|---|
| Professional business website — **$997 setup + $247/mo**, displayed separately per pricing rules | **Mockup-first**: the prospect sees a real mockup of *their* site before paying a dollar. Kills the pay-for-a-promise fear at the root | Live in **five days or less** — the site earns while agencies would still be scoping |
| Built and live in **five days or less** after yes | **Five days or less** vs. months. The proof is the calendar | Fixed, known cost — one setup fee, one monthly, no surprise invoices |
| Delivery, support, and billing 100% handled by OrenGen | Maintenance included in the $247/mo — the "web guy vanished" failure mode is structurally removed | A named human (their Sales Partner) plus a company behind them for delivery |
| Sold by a Sales Partner who shows, not tells | Fixed pricing, official checkout, no games — kills quote roulette | Site stays maintained without the owner ever touching it |

### Fit verdict: **STRONG FIT — PASS THE GATE**

The mechanism *is* the proof: the mockup demonstrates the exact deliverable, and the five-day promise is verifiable within one week of purchase. Pain relievers map one-to-one onto the four loudest pains (invisibility, abandonment, price fear, promise fear). Two conditions keep the verdict honest: **(1)** the five-days-or-less SLA must hold at volume — it is the load-bearing claim, so build capacity gets a metric and an owner per Mode 25; **(2)** mockup quality is the close — a weak mockup converts this from "showing the finished thing" to "showing a promise," which is the exact pain we claim to relieve.

## Part 3 — Pricing Decision Memo: the six [PROPOSED] services

Method per service: test the proposed price against the Mode 19 bands (subscription vault $29–$199/mo · premium bundle $197–$497 · done-with-you $997–$5,000+) and the §Money Math 3:1 LTV:CAC floor. Honest assumptions stated; where a scope fact is missing, the memo names the exact fact rather than inventing one. Mode 19 bands were written for digital products — for these services they are anchors, not law; the 3:1 floor *is* law. All six are backend offers sold into the owned OrenWeb customer base through checkout flows, so CAC is near-zero marginal — which means the gate is really fulfillment margin and scope honesty, not acquisition math.

**1. OrenWeb Talk — [PROPOSED $97/mo add-on] → NEED-[CONFIRM]-SCOPE.** $97/mo sits comfortably inside the subscription-vault band and reads clean against the $247/mo core (a 39% attach — an easy yes at checkout). As an attach to owned customers, CAC ≈ $0 and any positive gross margin clears 3:1 trivially. But the [CONFIRM] flag on this one is load-bearing: *chat widget only*, or *chat widget + voice answering*? If voice minutes are included, per-minute AI telephony cost at unknown usage could eat the margin at $97/mo, and the funnel positions this as "attach to every OrenWeb" — a thin-margin product attached to everything is a company-wide leak. **Fact needed before locking: exact scope (chat-only vs. chat+voice) and, if voice, the included-minutes cap and per-minute cost.** If chat-only: recommend $97/mo as proposed.

**2. AI Voice (Buy-Lingual™) — [PROPOSED $497 setup + $297/mo] → RECOMMEND.** The one proposed service with confirmed-enough scope (missed-call answering/recovery) and self-evident value logic: for most local service businesses, one recovered job covers the month. Assumptions, stated honestly: telephony + AI-minute COGS ~$60–$90/mo at typical local-business call volume → gross margin ~70%; average retention 12 months (defensible — the product's value is visible on every recovered call). LTV ≈ $297 × 0.70 × 12 ≈ $2,495 + setup margin; CAC ceiling ≈ $830 vs. a near-zero upsell CAC — clears 3:1 with room to spare. $297/mo exceeds the vault band but this is a service replacing answered-phone labor, not a digital product; the anchor that matters is the $247/mo core, and pricing the "recovers revenue" product above the "creates presence" product is coherent. The Spanish-line bump at [PROPOSED $97/mo] runs hot against the ≤30%-of-core bump rule — 33% of the monthly (over the line measured that way), ~12% of the first invoice ($794) — borderline; acceptable as a recurring companion. Lock it pending Andre's sign-off.

**3. AI Employees — [PROPOSED $997 setup + $497/mo] → NEED-[CONFIRM]-SCOPE.** The setup fee sits at the DWY band floor, which fits a configured back-office system. But $497/mo is the most expensive recurring line on the ladder — double the OrenWeb core — and the scope flag reads *"follow-up/booking/review agents?"* with a question mark. A price that says "worth 2× your website" needs a deliverable that displaces measurable labor, and neither the agent count, the functions, nor whether human QA rides along is on file. The proposed single-agent downsell [$297/mo] has the same problem: a lite tier can't be specced before the full tier is. **Facts needed: how many agents at $497/mo, which functions each performs, what human oversight is included, and the marginal cost of the [PROPOSED $197/mo] additional seat.** Price is plausible; scope is unwritten; the gate holds until it's written.

**4. OrenSocial — [PROPOSED $397/mo] → NEED-[CONFIRM]-SCOPE.** $397/mo lands in the premium-bundle band, sane for managed social. The economics fork entirely on the [CONFIRM]: *managed posting* (human labor per client — COGS plausibly 40–50%, GM ~55%, LTV ≈ $397 × 0.55 × 12 ≈ $2,620, still clears 3:1 on owned-list CAC but thin against any paid acquisition) vs. *content engine* (software-driven, GM 80%+, clears everything). The [PROPOSED $197/mo] posting-only downsell is only an honest lite tier if the $397 tier has named deliverables it subtracts from. **Facts needed: posts per month, platforms covered, who writes/approves content, and whether the review-response add-on [$97/mo] is automated or human.** No price recommendation until the deliverable count exists — copy can't say "we handle your social" unless fulfillment actually does.

**5. Better Together — [PROPOSED sum × 0.8, 12-mo commit] → ADJUST-TO: 0.8 on recurring only; setup fees at full price.** The structure is sound — 20% off for a 12-month commitment is a real exchange of value, not a fake discount (the customer trades flexibility, we trade margin; Handbook-clean). The math even favors us: a customer on OrenWeb + AI Voice pays $544/mo à la carte or $435.20/mo bundled × 12 committed months ≈ $5,222 of guaranteed recurring — if average standalone retention runs under 12 months, the bundle *raises* LTV while cutting GM roughly 62% from ~70%, and 3:1 still clears at owned-list CAC. The adjustment: setup fees are cost-recovery for real one-time labor (builds, configuration); discounting them 20% gives away margin that buys no retention, since retention is purchased by the commitment term, not the setup discount. Recur-only discounting also keeps the invoice display clean under the setup-vs-recurring separation rule. One prerequisite: the bundle can only include services whose own prices are locked — so this locks last.

**6. OrenNexus™ — [PROPOSED $497/mo + per-seat] → NEED-[CONFIRM]-SCOPE.** A white-label platform sold to agencies is a different business: B2B buyer, platform COGS per sub-account, and a per-seat component that is currently a phrase, not a price. $497/mo base is directionally credible for the agency-platform class, but the 3:1 floor is untestable with three unknowns: **(1) the per-seat price and what a "seat" is (sub-account? user login?); (2) the underlying platform cost per white-labeled account — the COGS that scales with every seat sold; (3) what the [PROPOSED $997] done-for-you onboarding bump actually includes in labor hours.** There is also a strategic fact to confirm before any copy ships: whether OrenNexus agencies can resell services that compete with our own rep channel — the answer shapes the whole funnel's enemy framing ("run your clients on your platform"). Highest ceiling on the ladder; furthest from lockable. Park it until the three numbers exist.
