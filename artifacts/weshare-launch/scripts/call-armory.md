# Call Armory — 10 Scripts (regenerated v2, 2026-07-08)

Source of record for GHL custom values + rep training. Extends Handbook §6 (the handbook stays the rep-facing canon; this is the ops copy). Voice: Trusted Counselor. Every script aims at one yes per call. Rep guardrail applies to all: sell the website ($997 + $247/mo) only — never commissions, upsells, or add-ons; no income/outcome claims; consented SMS only.

---

## 1 · Cold Opener (the named cold call)
> "Hey [Name], this is [Rep] — I'll be honest, this is a cold call, you can hang up on me in ten seconds. *(pause)* I was looking for [Business] online to get a quote and I couldn't really find you — or what I found looked like it hadn't been touched in a while. Are you the person who'd handle the website side of things?"

Branches: yes → §3 · "who is this" → "I build business websites for a company called OrenGen — but I'm not pitching yet. When I went to find you online, a customer probably couldn't either. Can I ask you one thing about that?" · "not interested" → "Understood — one thing before I go: when someone Googles [trade] in [town], do you come up, or does your competitor? That's the only reason I called."
**GHL:** outcome tag `Contacted` / `Not Interested` / `Callback [date]`.

## 2 · Gatekeeper
> "Hi — [Rep] with OrenGen. Quick one: who handles how [Business] shows up online — the website side? …Perfect. Is [Owner] the kind of person who'd want to know if customers can't find the business on Google, or should that go through you?"

Never pitch the gatekeeper; recruit them. If blocked twice: "Totally fair — I'll send one short email [Owner] can read in 30 seconds. What's the best address?"
**GHL:** tag `Gatekeeper — send email`, task next-day follow-up.

## 3 · Discovery Ladder (price the status quo)
1. "When a new customer wants to find you, what do they actually do?"
2. "When they Google [trade] in [town] — you, or someone else?"
3. "How are most new jobs coming in — referrals, repeat, or new?"
4. "What's one job worth to you, on average?"
5. "So if even two customers a month Google you, don't find you, and call the next guy — that's about $[2 × job value] walking out the door monthly. Low or high?"
6. "If I could show you what [Business] *should* look like online — free, no commitment, just a mockup — would you even want to see it?"

**GHL:** answers → notes; yes on #6 → tag `Mockup Requested`, stage → Appointment.

## 4 · The Mockup Close™ (call-one win)
> "Here's what I'd do — costs you nothing. My team builds a real mockup of your site — your business, your services, your town — and I walk you through it. Love it, we build the whole thing in five days or less. Don't love it, you keep the mockup and we part friends. Fair?"

Then: email + business details confirmed → tag `Mockup Requested`.

## 5 · Mockup Walkthrough (call two — the money call)
> "Before I show you — remember what you told me: [their #4/#5 answers, verbatim]. Keep that number in your head. *(share screen / send link)* This is [Business] the way a customer should meet it… *(walk 3 highlights max — the thing they said mattered first)* …So: $997 to build exactly this, live in five days or less, then $247 a month and we handle hosting, maintenance, updates, support — everything. Want it?"

Objections → Handbook §6.4 battlecards. Yes → send official checkout link on the call, stay on the line while they pay.
**GHL:** paid → stage Won (payment event drives commission) · thinking → tag `Proposal`, book a decision call before hanging up.

## 6 · Ghosted Follow-up (day 3)
> "[Name], [Rep] at OrenGen — your mockup's sitting here looking better than your competitor's actual website, and that's starting to feel wrong. Five minutes this week? Either way it's yours to keep."

**GHL:** two attempts max, then stage → Nurture.

## 7 · No-show Recovery
> "[Name] — no guilt, calendars eat everyone. The mockup's still ready and it's still free. I've got [two concrete slots]. Which is worse for you?"

One rebook only, then Nurture. Never a third chase — scarcity of the rep's time is real, act like it.

## 8 · Voicemail Set (rotate, never repeat)
- **VM1:** "It's [Rep] — I couldn't find [Business] online and I figure a customer can't either. I built you a free mockup of what it should look like. Call or text [company #] and I'll send it."
- **VM2 (day 3):** "[Rep] again — the mockup for [Business] is done. Takes five minutes to see. [Company #]."
- **VM3 (day 7, last):** "Last one from me, [Name] — the mockup stays yours either way. If timing's wrong, no problem. [Company #]."

## 9 · Referral Ask (post-launch, day ~10)
> "[Name], your site's live and looking right — congrats. One favor, if it earned it: who's one other business owner you know who's basically invisible online? I'll do for them exactly what I did for you — free mockup first, no strings. And if it's nobody, tell me straight."

**GHL:** referral → register as self-sourced prospect (rep owns it — first to register).

## 10 · The Save Call (fires from the cancellation alert)
> "[Name], it's [Rep] — I saw the site's coming down and I wanted to hear it from you, not from a report. What changed? *(listen — actually listen)* …If it's [fixable thing], we fix that this week. If it's money, tell me straight and let's see what the site actually brought in before we call it. And if it's truly done, I'll help make sure you don't lose your domain or your Google listing on the way out."

Rule: solve first, save second. A helped ex-client refers; a strong-armed one warns people.
**GHL:** outcome tag `Saved` / `Churned — [reason]`; log same-day.
