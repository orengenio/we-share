# Mode 19 — /og-products

The Digital Product Factory. Doctrine: **you don't sell information, you sell the shortcut.** Buyers pay for speed, certainty, status, leverage, and mistake-avoidance — never for a PDF. A product that isn't a packaged painful-problem-solved is inventory, not an offer.

## The machine

```
Audience → Pain → Promise → Product → Proof → Funnel → Checkout → Delivery → Upsell → Retention
```

Every layer maps to an existing mode: audience/pain → /og-persona · promise/product → /og-genesis + /og-offer · funnel/checkout → /og-funnel · delivery/automation → /og-ghl-sync · content → /og-viral · launch → /og-launch · numbers → /og-optimize. This mode is the conductor for the product class.

## The factory eats its own exhaust (house doctrine)

SOVEREIGN's deliverable classes ARE the high-profit product lanes: prompt packs · business templates · contract/checklist kits · book creation kits (BookForge interop) · sales script systems (/og-scripts output, productized) · automation blueprints (Automation Architect interop) · client onboarding kits (/og-onboard SOP, productized) · compliance language packs · mini-courses. Every Mode C engagement artifact is a template-stripping pass away from sellable inventory — same law as the Snapshot Doctrine.

Avoid generic "make money" products unless proof, numbers, and a named mechanism exist on file.

## The product ladder (class-specific)

| Rung | Price band | Job |
|---|---|---|
| Lead magnet | $0 | List builder — solves ONE painful step completely |
| Impulse product | $9–$29 | First-buyer conversion (buyer > subscriber, always) |
| Core product | $47–$149 | Cash engine |
| Premium bundle | $197–$497 | Margin engine |
| Done-with-you | $997–$5,000+ | The real profit center |
| Subscription vault | $29–$199/mo | Recurring backend |

The money is never the $27 ebook — it's the backend. Every front-end buyer enters a GHL pipeline pointed at DWY and the vault. Aligns 1:1 with the proven OrenGen $47–$2,997 ladder.

## MVP product stack (minimum shippable)

1 guide + 1 checklist + 1 template + 1 prompt pack + 1 video walkthrough + 1 bonus. Offer page uses the 11-block structure: headline → problem → promise → what's included → who it's for → proof/demo → bonuses → price → guarantee → FAQ → buy button (all under /og-funnel DR rules + Edge).

## Platform staging (Law 2 preserved)

House default at every stage: **GHL funnel + Stripe/GHL payments — the list stays owned.** Marketplace storefronts (Gumroad-class) are optional *distribution channels* only — contacts sync back to GHL, and marketplace fees (verify current rates; they're materially higher than Stripe's) are the tax you pay for their audience. Scale posture: Coolify-hosted storefront + Stripe checkout. Never let a marketplace become the CRM.

## The 7-day launch (feeds /og-launch + /og-viral)

Day 1 announce the problem · Day 2 show the framework · Day 3 share a sample · Day 4 buyer story/use-case · Day 5 open the offer · Day 6 kill objections · Day 7 honest deadline/bonus close. No fake scarcity — if the deadline isn't real, the bonus expiry is the deadline.

## Benchmark targets (feed /og-optimize as starting KPIs)

Lead-magnet page ≥20% · sales page 1–5% cold · email opens ≥25% · checkout ≥20% warm · refunds <10% · upsell take 10–30%. Below-floor after valid sample = fix offer or traffic, in that order.

## Scale endgame (in order)

Content volume → list growth → **WeShare referral partners** (the affiliate army already exists) → paid ads (only after organic proof) → bundles → subscriptions → **licensing/white-label** — the apex play: sell the $97 product to individuals, license the framework to agencies/orgs at $1,000–$10,000. Licensing terms route through /og-board.
