# OrenGen Sales Partner Handbook
**Rules of Engagement · Standard Operating Procedures · v1.1 (July 2026)**

You are a 100% commission closer for OrenGen Worldwide. We hand you the leads, the scripts, the CRM, and a company number. You run the conversations and close. We build, deliver, and support everything after. This handbook is the complete operating manual.

---

## 1 · The Offer You Sell

Professional business website (new or rebuilt): **$997 setup + $247/month** maintenance, hosting, and support. Pricing is fixed. Payment is collected **only** through OrenGen's official checkout.

## 2 · Your Compensation

| Stream | Rate | Amount | Paid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upfront | 25% of setup | **$249.25 per close** | After client's payment clears |
| Residual | 25% of maintenance | **$61.75/mo per client — for life of the client** | Each month the client pays |

- **Payable when funds clear.** A close isn't a commission until the client's money lands.
- **Payouts:** weekly, every Friday, via Stripe Connect (onboard from your dashboard — required). $25 minimum rolls forward. Stripe issues your 1099.
- **Clawback:** client refund within 30 days reverses the upfront as a negative ledger entry on your next payout.
- **Residuals are payment-driven:** client skips or churns → that month doesn't pay. Save the client, save the income.
- If a WeShare Referral Partner sourced the lead, their commission comes from OrenGen's share — **your rates never change.**

## 3 · Onboarding SOP

1. **Apply** → screening interview (we're a curated roster, not an open door).
2. **Sign** the Sales Partner Agreement + complete W-9/Stripe Connect.
3. **Provisioning:** CRM seat (GoHighLevel), your assigned OrenGen phone number, script library, objection guide.
4. **Certification:** one recorded role-play close with OrenGen leadership. Pass = leads unlock.
5. **First leads assigned.** You're live.

## 4 · Lead Lifecycle SOP

1. **Assignment** — leads appear in your CRM pipeline with source tag (house / referral partner / self-sourced).
2. **First touch within 4 business hours.** Speed is the single biggest close-rate lever.
3. **Cadence:** minimum **6 touches across 10 business days** — call, voicemail, SMS, email, mixed. Use the scripts.
4. **Status discipline:** every lead sits in exactly one stage — New → Contacted → Appointment → Proposal → Won / Lost / Nurture. Update same day, every day.
5. **Recycle rules:** no first touch in **48 hours** → lead auto-returns to the pool. No activity for **7 days** on an open lead → recycled. Recycled leads carry no commission claim.
6. **Close:** send the official OrenGen checkout link. Never collect payment any other way.
7. **Handoff checklist (same day as close):** client name, business, domain status, design notes, and expectations call booked with fulfillment. Clean handoffs protect your residual.

## 5 · Sales Call Rules

- Sell from the approved scripts and claims sheet. You may flex tone and order — never facts.
- **No discounts, freebies, or custom terms** without written approval from OrenGen leadership.
- Never promise delivery dates, features, or outcomes beyond the approved claims sheet.
- Set the maintenance expectation on every close: $247/month, ongoing — surprise billing kills retention and your residual.

## 6 · Sales Scripts & Objection Battlecards

These are your approved scripts. You may flex tone and order — never the facts or the price (§5). Every call aims at **one yes: permission to send a free mockup.** That is the win on call one, not the sale. The mockup does the selling; you just earn the right to send it.

**Voice:** trusted counselor, not telemarketer. Consultative, warm, direct — a peer who noticed something, never hypey. Lead with *their invisibility* online, not our product.

### 6.1 Cold Call Opener

> "Hey [Name], this is [Rep] — I'll be honest, this is a cold call, you can hang up on me in ten seconds. *(pause)* I was looking for [Business] online to get a quote and I couldn't really find you — or what I found looked like it hadn't been touched in a while. Are you the person who'd handle the website side of things?"

- **If yes** → "Cool. Quick question and then I'll get out of your hair —" → go to Discovery.
- **If "who is this / what's this about"** → "Totally fair. I build business websites for a company called OrenGen — but I'm not here to pitch you. I called because when I went to find you online, a customer probably couldn't either. Can I ask you one thing about that?"
- **If "not interested"** → "Understood — before I go, real quick: when someone Googles [their trade] in [town], does your business come up, or does your competitor's? *(pause)* …That's the only reason I called."

*Edge move: name the cold call out loud (it disarms), then lead with their invisibility, not our service.*

### 6.2 Discovery Ladder

Surface the pain in order, then price the status quo:

1. "When a new customer wants to find you, what do they actually do — call, Google, ask a friend?"
2. "And when they Google [trade] in [town] — do you show up, or does someone else?"
3. "How are most of your new jobs coming in right now — referrals, repeat, or new?"
4. "What's one job worth to you on average?" *(anchor the math)*
5. "So if even **two** customers a month are Googling you, not finding you, and calling the next guy — that's roughly $[2 × job value] walking out the door monthly. Does that number feel low or high to you?"
6. "If I could show you exactly what your business *should* look like online — no charge, no commitment, just a mockup — is that something you'd even want to see?"

The whole call is aimed at #6: a yes to receiving a mockup.

### 6.3 The Mockup Close (the only close that matters on call one)

> "Here's what I'd do — and this costs you nothing. My team will build a real mockup of your site — your business, your services, your town — and I'll walk you through it. If you love it, we build the whole thing in five days or less. If you don't, you keep the mockup and we part friends. Fair?"

Then: get their **email + confirm business details** → tag `Mockup Requested` in CRM → move the opportunity to **Appointment**.

### 6.4 Objection Battlecards

*Acknowledge in their words → reframe the root fear → give proof → ask a close question.*

**"I already have a website / a Facebook page."**
> "Good — a lot of my clients did too. When's the last time it actually *brought* you a job, versus just existing? *(pause)* A Facebook page is a rental — you don't own it and it doesn't show up on Google the same way. Let me send the mockup and put them side by side. Worst case you confirm yours is fine. Deal?"

**"How much is it?"** *(price, asked early)*
> "Straight answer: $997 to build, then $247 a month to host, maintain, update, and keep it ranking — no surprise fees. But I never ask anyone to decide on price before they've seen what they're getting. Let me show you the mockup first, *then* the number's an easy yes or an easy no. Cool?"

**"I don't have time for this."**
> "That's exactly why I do it this way — *you* do nothing. My team builds it, I bring it to you, it's live in five days. The time cost is one 10-minute look at the mockup. That's the whole ask."

**"I need to think about it." / "Send me some info."**
> "Totally — and the best thing to think *about* is the actual thing, not a brochure. So let me get the mockup made — that IS the info — and I'll follow up [day]. If it's a no after you see it, it's a clean no. What email should it go to?"

**"Is this a scam? / How do I know you're real?"**
> "Ask that — you should. OrenGen Worldwide is a real US company, you'll get everything in writing, and you don't pay a cent until you've seen the site and said yes. I'll send it from our company email so you can check us out. Skepticism is smart — let the mockup earn the trust."

**"I tried something like this before and got burned."**
> "Then you already know the difference between someone who disappears and someone who ships. Here's mine: five days to live, in writing, and you see it before you pay. What went wrong last time — so I make sure it doesn't here?"

**"Business is fine / I'm booked out."**
> "Love that — and the best time to fix the roof is when it's not raining. When the phone slows down, the guys who show up online first are the ones who stay booked. Cheap insurance against a slow month. Let me at least show you the mockup."

**"Just email me and I'll look later."**
> "Done — but let's make it worth opening: I'll have the mockup made so you're looking at your *actual* site, not a pitch. Best email? And is [day] a bad day for me to check you got it?"

### 6.5 Follow-Up Templates

Every SMS requires prior consent and carries an opt-out. Stay inside the 8 AM–9 PM local-time window (§7).

**SMS — after the mockup is requested**
> "Hi [Name], [Rep] from OrenGen. Great talking — building your site mockup now, you'll have it by [day]. Reply STOP to opt out."

**SMS — mockup ready**
> "[Name], your [Business] mockup is ready — took me 5 min to see why customers would pick you. When's good for a quick look today or tomorrow? Reply STOP to opt out."

**SMS — ghosted (day 3)**
> "[Name], didn't want your mockup to get buried — it's built and it's yours either way. Worth 5 min? Reply STOP to opt out."

**Email — cold recap**
> **Subject:** The reason customers can't find [Business]
> "[Name] — following up from my call. When I searched [trade] in [town], your competitor came up and you didn't. I'm having my team build a free mockup of what [Business] *should* look like online — no cost, no commitment. Reply 'yes' and I'll send it over. — [Rep], OrenGen"

**Email — post-mockup win-back (day 7)**
> **Subject:** Keeping the mockup or should I close the file?
> "[Name], your [Business] site mockup is still sitting here. No pressure — just don't want to keep it open if the timing's wrong. Want the 5-minute walkthrough, or should I close it out? Either's fine. — [Rep]"

**Voicemail**
> "[Name], it's [Rep] — I called because I couldn't find [Business] online and figured a customer probably can't either. No pitch — I just built you a free mockup of what it should look like. Call or text me back at [number] and I'll send it. Thanks."

### 6.6 What You Never Say

- No income or earnings claims to prospects. No outcome guarantees ("you'll get X customers").
- No fake urgency ("only 3 spots"), no invented discounts.
- **Never discuss commissions, upsells, or any add-on products.** You sell the website — **$997 + $247/mo**. Full stop.
- Never send an unbranded link. Never text without consent. Honor every opt-out instantly.

## 7 · Communication Compliance (Non-Negotiable)

- Contact prospects **only between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM in the prospect's local time.**
- **Honor every opt-out instantly** — "stop," "remove me," "don't call" ends outreach to that contact permanently. Mark it in CRM same day.
- Use **your assigned OrenGen number** for all prospect calls and texts — never your personal line. It keeps you compliant and keeps records clean.
- No purchased lists, no auto-dialers, no bulk SMS blasts. You work the leads we assign and the leads you legitimately self-source.
- If a call is recorded, follow the disclosure script. When in doubt, disclose.

## 8 · CRM Hygiene

Same-day logging of every touch. Required fields on every won deal: opportunity value, contact info verified, Stripe customer ID, source tag. Your CRM record is the system of record for your commissions — if it isn't logged, it didn't happen.

## 9 · Conduct & Integrity

- **Clients belong to OrenGen.** Taking or steering a client (or any OrenGen lead) to yourself or a third party = immediate termination + forfeiture + clawback of related commissions.
- No off-platform payments, side deals, or personal upsells to OrenGen clients.
- You may not sell competing website/maintenance services to leads OrenGen provided.
- Confidentiality: lead data, scripts, pricing strategy, and this program's internals are OrenGen property. Lead exports are prohibited.
- Represent OrenGen professionally. You carry the brand on every call.

## 10 · Client Lifecycle After the Close

Fulfillment delivers; support retains. You'll see your book of clients and monthly residual run-rate live in your dashboard. If a client signals cancellation, you're notified — a save call from the person who sold them is the highest-percentage retention play, and it's your income on the line.

## 11 · Leaving the Program

- **Exit in good standing** (resignation, inactivity): your **lifetime residuals continue** on every client you closed, as promised. You earned them.
- **Termination for cause** (fraud, circumvention, compliance breach): forfeits unpaid and future commissions.
- Inactive 60+ days → lead assignments pause; your residuals are untouched.

## 12 · Enforcement & Disputes

Strike system for minor breaches (CRM neglect, cadence misses): written strike + cure period; three strikes = removal from lead rotation, then termination. Compliance and integrity violations (§6, §8) skip strikes. Disputes: file from your dashboard within 30 days of statement; ledger-traced ruling in writing within 5 business days.
